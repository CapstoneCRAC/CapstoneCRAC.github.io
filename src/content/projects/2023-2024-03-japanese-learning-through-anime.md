This web app uses anime as a familiar visual theme for people beginning to learn Japanese. The learning journey starts with Hiragana and Katakana, presenting written characters alongside Romanised pronunciation and a button that plays the corresponding sound. Voiced, half-voiced, voiceless and Yōon sounds are included as part of this foundation.

The vocabulary section then connects Japanese words with three selected anime backgrounds. It includes 60 words with Chinese meanings and audio playback, choosing practical vocabulary that can also appear in daily life. A final quiz draws 20 items at random from a 60-question pool. Learners receive clear green or red answer feedback but no timer or score pressure, so they can keep trying at their own pace.

## From scripts to quiz

- Hiragana and Katakana tables with Romanisation and audio
- Coverage of voiced, half-voiced, voiceless and Yōon sounds
- 60 anime-themed vocabulary items with Chinese meanings and pronunciation
- Three vocabulary pages with distinct anime backgrounds
- A 20-question random quiz drawn from a 60-question pool

## What was completed

The report documents three completed learning sections: script practice, vocabulary study and a quiz. Additional anime themes, new graphics and language-pair options are identified as future work.
