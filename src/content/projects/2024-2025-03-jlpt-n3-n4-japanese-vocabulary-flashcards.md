This web app supports intermediate Japanese learners preparing for JLPT N3 and N4 vocabulary. Learners choose a level, browse a word list and open individual flashcards that bring together kanji, romaji, meaning, word class, an emoji cue and Japanese audio. The app is designed as a lightweight, browser-based alternative for independent review.

Navigation supports both planned and spontaneous practice. The word list shows 20 items at a time, while Previous, Next and Random Word controls let learners keep moving through the material. A browse-history feature remembers up to ten recently viewed words, so a learner can quickly return to vocabulary that needs another look or listen. The responsive layout works on desktop and mobile browsers, keeping the vocabulary library easy to access.

## Key features

- Separate JLPT N3 and N4 vocabulary sets with 208 and 127 entries
- Flashcards with kanji, romaji, meaning, word class and emoji cues
- Auto-played Japanese pronunciation with a replay control
- Previous, Next and Random Word navigation
- Browse history for up to ten recently viewed words

## Project outcome

The completed web app was evaluated by ten classmates on Chrome and Safari. The report records an average usability score of 8.5/10 and notes minor mobile-Safari audio delays as a future optimisation area.
