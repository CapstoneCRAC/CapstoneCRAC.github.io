The proposal’s central image is a tongue that can be seen and moved — an answer to pronunciation errors that are often felt but hard to locate. The final report narrows that broad three-dimensional ambition into a browser activity for flat and retroflex contrasts, where a learner hears a syllable, positions a movable tongue and tests the choice against the mouth diagram.

The reported build focuses on z/zh, c/ch and s/sh. Its drag limits make the articulation position matter, while timed rounds, replay and touch or mouse input give repeated practice a game rhythm. Nasal contrasts, voice recording and personalised paths remain named future possibilities, not features the page claims are already delivered.

## See where the tongue should go

- A dynamic oral-cavity diagram with a movable tongue
- Timed ten-question challenges built around target syllables
- Visual validation of flat and retroflex tongue positions
- Mouse and touch input for browser use
- Victory and restart feedback after each round

## Delivered scope

The final report describes a fully functional browser application covering flat and retroflex contrasts.
