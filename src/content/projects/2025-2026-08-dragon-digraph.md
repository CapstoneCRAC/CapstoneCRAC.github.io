Dragon Digraph is a browser-based learning adventure for children aged 4–9. Each island introduces one of five common consonant digraphs — sh, ch, th, wh and ck — through a simple visual journey. Children choose a dragon companion, travel across the island map and work through activities designed to move from seeing and hearing a sound to recognising and saying it.

The app combines four kinds of practice. Learn Mode presents image-word pairs with slow text-to-speech; the Digraph Quiz checks recognition; Drag & Drop adds a hands-on spelling task; and Say It invites learners to speak the target word for immediate animated feedback. Completion status and dragon rewards make progress visible as children unlock the next stage.

## Key features

- Five digraph islands: sh, ch, th, wh and ck
- Learn Mode with picture-word pairs and slow spoken models
- Multiple-choice quiz and drag-and-drop spelling activities
- Say It speech-recognition practice with “good job” or “try again” feedback
- Avatar choice, island progress and dragon rewards stored on the device

## Project outcome

The final report documents a completed single-page web app with all five islands and four activity types. Content is stored in JSON so future digraphs, word lists and thresholds can be updated without rewriting the core app.
