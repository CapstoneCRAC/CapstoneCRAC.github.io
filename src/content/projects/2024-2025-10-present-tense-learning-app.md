This learning app focuses on the difference between present tense and present continuous tense. Learners begin with definitions and example sentences, then move into exercises that ask them to recognise and apply the two forms. Chinese meanings, visual cues and immediate response sounds are included to make the grammar easier to approach in context.

The app adds a small reward loop to the practice routine. Learners who complete an exercise with fewer than three mistakes earn in-app money, which can be spent on collectible items in a shop and viewed later in My Bag. Seven exercises and a final exam provide a route through the material while encouraging learners to keep returning to the tense practice. The activities are designed for short, repeatable study sessions.

## Key features

- Present-tense and present-continuous explanations with examples
- Seven exercises and a final exam
- Chinese meanings, visuals and correct/incorrect sound feedback
- Encouraging messages after each answer
- A reward shop and My Bag collection linked to exercise completion

## Project outcome

The report describes a working learning app built around a 302-verb list. The original virtual-pet idea was simplified to the current shop-and-collectibles reward system.
