This website was created for students who know they need to reply to an email but are unsure how to begin, structure the message or judge the tone. It brings together examples and advice drawn from familiar situations, giving users a simple starting point for writing replies that are clear, polite and appropriate.

The reported examples cover situations such as asking for a syllabus, responding to a professor, applying for an internship and confirming a meeting time. The site is intended to be a study and planning aid rather than an automatic email writer: learners can examine the structure and wording of a sample before adapting the idea to their own purpose. An editable on-page email box was planned but was not completed.

## Key features

- Example replies for common school and workplace situations
- Local, student-focused guidance on format and polite tone
- Scenarios including professor, internship, syllabus and meeting replies
- A read-only sample-email view for studying structure and wording

## Project outcome

The report documents a working website with example content. It explicitly states that the planned editable email box was not completed, so the public page does not promise in-page customisation or instant writing feedback.
