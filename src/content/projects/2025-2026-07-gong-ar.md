The pitch begins with an AR challenge: collect a topic’s words, listen to their Chinese and Jyutping cues, then speak them through a red-to-green barrier. The final report takes that wish for immediate pronunciation feedback into a different, browser-based form — short Cantonese sentences, a six-tone ladder and a recording button.

Users choose an easy or medium sentence, record in the browser and compare what the system hears with the target Jyutping. Correct and incorrect syllables point to an initial, final or tone that needs another attempt; model and learner recordings sit side by side. A searchable sentence set makes it possible to return to one troublesome item rather than treating the activity as a one-time quiz.

## Record, compare, repeat

- Easy and medium Cantonese sentence practice with Jyutping
- A six-tone ladder for visualising pitch patterns
- Browser recording with a live volume display
- ASR-based comparison of target and heard Jyutping
- Syllable-level feedback for initials, finals and tones

## Accuracy limits

The report documents a publicly deployed web application with a sentence dataset of fewer than 80 items. It notes that background noise, speaking speed and the current ASR model can affect feedback accuracy.
