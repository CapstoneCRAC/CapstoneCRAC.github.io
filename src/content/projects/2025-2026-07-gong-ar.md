This browser-based practice tool is for learners beginning Cantonese pronunciation. Users select an easy or medium sentence, see its Jyutping and a six-tone ladder, then record themselves directly in the browser. The activity is designed around short, repeatable sentence practice rather than a general vocabulary course.

After recording, the app compares the transcription with the target Jyutping and highlights correct and incorrect syllables. Learners can see where an initial, final or tone needs attention, alongside both the model pronunciation and their recorded audio. A searchable sentence set and a welcome guide help users choose material and return to specific practice items. The feedback directs the next attempt toward a specific part of the syllable and makes focused repetition more practical.

## Key features

- Easy and medium Cantonese sentence practice with Jyutping
- A six-tone ladder for visualising pitch patterns
- Browser recording with a live volume display
- ASR-based comparison of target and heard Jyutping
- Syllable-level feedback for initials, finals and tones

## Project outcome

The report documents a publicly deployed web application with a sentence dataset of fewer than 80 items. It notes that background noise, speaking speed and the current ASR model can affect feedback accuracy.
