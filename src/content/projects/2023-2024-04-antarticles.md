ANTARTICLES approaches English articles as part of a connected learning process rather than an isolated rule. Across 15 levels, learners meet themed nouns, decide which article fits, build sentences and revisit the idea through related tasks. The structure is designed to move gradually from recognition to use, including later attention to exceptions.

Audio is part of the learning path from the start: spoken prompts and read-aloud support are built into warm-up, task and recap moments. Instead of a leaderboard, the app uses manuscripts as tutorial-style hints that help a learner move through a difficult point. Completing the main levels also unlocks a typing-mode review activity, creating another way to return to article–noun pairings without a time limit.

## A 15-level learning path

- Fifteen progressive levels for article learning
- Four task stages that connect nouns, articles and sentence construction
- Spoken prompts, read-aloud support and recap audio
- Manuscript-style hints that scaffold difficult moments
- An unlockable typing-mode review activity

## Built scope

The final report documents a completed 15-level web app with its core task structure, hint system, audio support and review activity. Future work proposes adapting the framework to German articles.
