This project examines the potential of an AI chatbot to support people buying, renting or selling property on Lamma Island. The report frames the chatbot as an always-available first point of contact: users could ask about listings, local amenities, transport and neighbourhood life, then be connected with a human agent when the conversation reaches a more complex decision.

The proposed experience focuses on simple, practical requests. A user could filter listings by price, location or bedrooms, clarify property details or arrange a viewing. The evaluation also keeps the limits of automation in view: participants valued quick answers and appointment support, but raised questions about usability, privacy, accuracy and the need for human agents in negotiations and complex transactions. Human judgement remains central when a property decision becomes sensitive or complex.

## Proposed conversational support

- Conversational property search by price, location and bedroom count
- Listing details, local-area information and viewing support
- Routing from simple questions to human real-estate agents
- Support ideas for tenant communication and maintenance requests
- Consideration of privacy, security and accuracy in chatbot use

## Evidence and limits

The report is primarily an evaluation of the chatbot concept, using surveys and interviews. The page does not claim that a public production service or all proposed property-management functions are available.
